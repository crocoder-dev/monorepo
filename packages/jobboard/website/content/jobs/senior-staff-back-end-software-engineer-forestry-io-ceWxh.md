## About Forestry:

At Forestry.io, we believe the web is one of humanity’s greatest achievements and our mission is to help people build a better web. We’re building a Git-backed content management system ([Forestry CMS](https://forestry.io/docs/welcome/)) and an open source site-editing toolkit ([Tina CMS](https://tina.io/docs/)) -both focused on the JAMstack.We're now [launching Tina Cloud](https://tina.io/), the next-generation content management system that takes the best of Forestry CMS & Tina CMS. It's an open-core content management solution with visual editing and a GraphQL API backed by Git.[We’re a growing company](https://forestry.io/about) with big dreams that's full of opportunities for you to have an impact and grow with us. Come and help us build the future of content management.

## The Role:

We're looking for a Senior (or Staff-level) Back-end Software Engineer to help us build Tina Cloud. We believe [Tina](https://tina.io/) will be a game-changer for content management on the web, and you'll be instrumental in architecting and evolving the system to meet the needs of our users.

Because you are a product-minded developer, you easily connect the dots between our tech, the business and of course, our users. You are empathetic, thoughtful and a true team player, collaborating easily with your colleagues across the org. You love the fast pace of startups, the ownership that comes with being a part of a small team and are passionate about open source software and communities. If you're an optimistic self-starter who loves finding elegant solutions to hard problems, we want to hear from you!

What You'll Do:

*   Contribute to the vision and technical strategy of Tina Cloud.
*   Architect and build the APIs that power the developers using Tina.
*   Help establish high technical standards for the team.
*   Provide technical mentorship to other engineers on the team.

Who You Are:

*   You have 10+ years of software development experience, and have built and scaled multiple production back-end systems before.
*   You have experience architecting systems on cloud infrastructure.
*   You’re excellent at weighing the short and long-term tradeoffs of complex technical decisions.
*   You’re an excellent communicator and collaborator, driving technical decisions in a collaborative environment.
*   You enjoy digging deep and solving tough problems with code, as you’ll be building abstractions, refactoring and troubleshooting regularly.
*   You enjoy the autonomy, entrepreneurialism and bias for execution found in a technology startup.
*   Our tech stack includes React, Go, Node.js & AWS. The more experience you have with these technologies, the better, but not required.

Nice-To-Haves:

*   You’ve worked on building APIs or libraries for other developers and understand the importance of a great DX.
*   You have experience building content management systems.
*   You have experience working with the technologies in the JAMstack ecosystem.
*   You have deep expertise with cloud architecture, including security, containerization & serverless.
